About us

Who are we ?

We are a High School located between the Umfuleni and Khalistha Townships, inside a semi-township known as Drift Sands, which was previously an informal settlement.

What do we do ?

We started off as a primary school, only attending to grades ranging from 1-7, but due to the sudden growth of population we have been forced to accomodate all grades from 1 to matric.


Mzamomtsha School is in the remote township of Driftsands. The school has a severe shortage of classrooms and chairs. This follows an early 2018 instruction from the district office for the school to accommodate children who could not fit into overcrowded Mfuleni schools. The Department of Education promised the school mobile classes to help handle the increased learner population, but these have still had not been delivered.

Parents organised a protest to highlight the school's needs, but nothing has changed. Frustrations are growing, and parents are even contemplating closing down the school permanently as they feel the learning conditions their children are enduring are unacceptable.

FAQs

How many pupils per class in the school?

  • There are currently 60 learners per classroom, but it should be expected to decrease with the introduction of mobile classrooms provided by the Western Cape Education Department.

Do the children wear uniforms?

  • Yes, uniforms are worn from grade 1 through to matric at Mzamomtsha Primary School.
